History of the estate

Year of creation: 1890s Authors: The Aseev family. History of creation At the beginning of the 20th century, Mikhail Aseev acquired a plot of land in Tambov with an old park and a house portrait painter Nikolai Shevchenko. By 1905, according to the design of architect Lev Kekushev, it was built An Art Nouveau palace. In his work, the architect managed to create an integral ensemble of interiors with unique elements: fine stucco, paintings, original panels, lighting fixtures, built-in furniture and wooden finishing canvases. The Aseev family belonged to an influential merchant dynasty. The Aseevs owned textile factories, They were engaged in charity work: they built schools, maintained hospitals and shelters. For services to the Fatherland Mikhail Aseev was granted hereditary nobility. History of use after the revolution In the spring of 1918, the estate was nationalized, and the Aseev family emigrated. After the departure of the owners in The manor was occupied for some time by mounted soldiers. Over the years, the building housed: summer colony for homeless children; orphanage (1920s); tuberculosis, and later — a cardiological sanatorium. During the Great Patriotic War, the somatic sanatorium was closed, and the building was transferred to a hospital. People's Commissariat of Health. Restoration and opening as a museum In 2009, the restoration of the estate began. The work was carried out with a thorough study of archival materials, photos and descriptions. The restorers restored the facades with decorative moldings and stained glass windows, the front door stairs and interior interiors, parquet floors and fireplaces, elements of artistic interior decoration. On September 27, 2014, the grand opening of the restored Aseev Manor took place, dedicated to the founding day of the Tambov region. More than 400 million rubles were allocated for the restoration of the monument . Today, the museum complex is the cultural center of Tambov and the region. Charity events are held here. concerts, exhibitions, creative meetings, chamber music evenings. Russian subjects are represented in the estate. and Western European decorative and applied arts, porcelain, painting, furniture of the XVII — early XX century. There are also specialized exhibitions, such as "The History and fate of the Aseev family" and "N. M. Shevchenko, an artist and friend of M. V. Aseev." The Aseev Estate has the status of a branch of the Peterhof Museum-Reserve.

The view of visitors

This plan is built not as a dry inspection of the halls, but as an immersive immersion into life. dynasties. Instead of a list of exhibits, there is a logic of moving in terms of meanings: from a demonstration of status to an intimate private one. lives. 🏛 The concept: "From brilliance to soul" 1️. Exterior and front entrance: A "Statement to the World" - The goal: To feel the scale of the family's influence. - What to look at: Architectural eclecticism. The estate was supposed to broadcast: "We are not just industrialists, we are — a part of European culture." - The feeling: Grandeur, order, the transition from industrial Chelyabinsk to the world of high aesthetics. 2️. Grand staircase and lobby: "Entrance ritual" - Goal: To understand the social role of the house. - What to look at: The proportions of the staircase, the details of the decoration. It is a space for receiving guests, where every step is he emphasized the status of the host. - Feeling: Awe of space, preparation for the encounter with art. 3️. Art Gallery: "The heart of the collection" - The goal: To understand the taste of the Aseyevs. - What to look at: Not just the paintings, but the way they are combined. The Aseev collection is a manifesto. Looking for a connection between painting, architecture, and the way light falls on canvases. - Meaning: Here the family "talked" with world masters. This is not a warehouse of things, but a personal dialogue with beauty. 4️. Music and reading rooms: "Intellectual comfort" - The goal: To see the "human face" of the dynasty. - What to look at: Musical instruments, bookshelves, chamber furniture. These are the zones where art ceases to be It becomes an "object of contemplation" and becomes a "way of life." - Feeling: Warmth, closeness, silence. Here, the Aseevs were not patrons, but listeners and readers. 5️. Private rooms and an office: "Secrets and solutions" - The goal: To understand the value of this greatness. - What to look at: Desktop, personal belongings. The place where decisions were made that influenced the development of the city and art. - Feeling: Concentration of will and personal responsibility. 6️. Finale: Garden and walk: "Eternal Return" - Purpose: Reflection. - Action: Exit to the garden. - Meaning: The art created within these walls continues to live in the atmosphere of the manor. A closed loop from the external splendor to the inner peace of nature. , Advice for the visitor: Don't try to do everything in time. Choose one room (preferably a gallery) and spend 15 minutes in it, trying to imagine that you — a guest invited to Aseev's dinner.

The view of visitors

Stage 1: Preparation (Before leaving home) - Booking: The best experience at the Aseev Estate is a guided tour. It connects disparate halls into a single one. the story. Book it in advance on the website or by phone. - Time check: Specify the work schedule (the estate may have different hours for the halls and for the park). - Budget: Decide in advance whether you are taking a ticket to the general exhibition or to a special exhibition. 🏛 Stage 2: Arrival and external inspection - Architectural emphasis: Before entering, inspect the facade. Pay attention to the details of the eclectic style — these are it will help you adjust to the "historical mood". - The courtyard/Entrance: Take a few shots at the main entrance to capture the beginning of the walk. Stage 3: Main route (Inside the museum) 1. Art Gallery (Main goal): - Head here first, while you have concentration. - What to do: Study painting (from classics to modernism) and sculpture. If you are walking without a guide, use audio guide or carefully read the explications (signs). 2. Music Salon: - Go to the tools. This is an area with special acoustics and atmosphere. - What to do: Listen to the descriptions of rare instruments. Try to "hear" the music in the silence of the hall. 3. Historical halls and library: - Get acquainted with the life of the Aseev family. - What to do: Pay attention to the details of the interior (furniture, decor), which explain the level of wealth and the interests of the owners. 4. Finishing in the garden/park: - After a rich visual program, you need to "unload" your eyes. - What to do: Take a walk around the estate. It's the perfect place to reflect on what you've seen. Stage 4: Post-visit - Souvenirs: Visit the museum shop (if it is open) for high-quality printing or memorabilia. - Photo report: View the pictures taken in the halls (if photography is allowed) and select the best ones. , The user's checklist: - Sign up for a guided tour. - Check for temporary exhibitions (they may be more interesting than the main collection). - Take a break for 10-15 minutes in a quiet room so that there is no "overload effect" from the abundance of art. - Clarify the rules of photography (with or without flash). , Recommended time: 1.5 — 2 hours.
